# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mobile players who want a short, tactile, one-handed strategy game that feels cozy at the surface and rewards careful optimization underneath. They play in 8-15 minute sessions, often on a phone in portrait orientation, and need the game to remain pausable, readable, and comfortable without real-time pressure.

## Product Purpose

Lucky Shelf is a cozy spatial roguelite about running a tiny shop. Each day, players draft items, arrange them on a shelf grid, and open the shop to earn coins from deterministic adjacency combos. Success means the player understands why every coin was earned, cares deeply about where each item sits, and wants to replay for better layouts, stronger combos, deeper rent survival, catalog discoveries, and daily-shelf comparison.

## Brand Personality

Warm, tactile, and clever. The game should feel like a golden-hour general store made of paper, wood, gouache sprites, small rituals, and satisfying object handling, while the underlying economy stays crunchy enough for ruthless optimization.

## Anti-references

Do not look or feel like a casino, gacha, idle game, management sim, or generic mobile F2P economy. Avoid jackpot flashes, mystery-box pressure, passive earnings, permanent power creep, hidden scoring, excessive real-time pressure, and systems that make shelf placement less important than menus, upgrades, or business management.

## Design Principles

1. Placement is the verb: every feature should make the player care where an item goes.
2. Every coin is explained: scoring must be traceable, animatable, and auditable from the `ScoringTrace`.
3. Cozy outside, crunchy inside: warmth and tactility should invite play, but the systems should reward sharp spatial planning.
4. Phone sessions stay respectful: portrait, one-handed, pausable, readable, and fast enough for short runs.
5. Determinism is a product feature: seeded runs, replayable decisions, dailies, ghosts, and share artifacts should build from deterministic state and action logs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Primary controls belong in the bottom reach zone, touch targets should be at least 44 pt, and the game should support reduced motion, adjustable haptics, colorblind-safe rule indicators, high-contrast/readable scoring states, and simple item text that does not rely on lore-first phrasing.
